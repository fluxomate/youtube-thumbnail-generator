# Style profile

_The skill reads this before generating every concept and image. Keep it current._

## Channel / niche
**Fluxomate** (creator: Leo). Tagline: "Building scale-proof agencies one system at a
time." Content is AI-powered automation for agencies — CRM builds, Zapier/workflow
systems, tools like ChurnBuster — aimed at SMM/marketing agency owners who want to
scale with systems instead of hiring. Audience: agency founders/operators, automation-
curious entrepreneurs. Tone: practical, build-in-public, high-value/no-fluff.
(Confirm/refine with creator — pulled from public channel info, not the live video list.)

## Persona on camera
Young man, Mediterranean/Middle-Eastern features, warm friendly smile. Short, neatly
cropped dark hair (faded sides) and a full, well-groomed dark beard. Two looks:
- **Clean:** no glasses, fitted orange t-shirt — polished studio headshot vibe.
- **Creator/vlog:** black thick-framed square glasses, orange hoodie — casual, talking
  to camera, expressive hand gestures.
Approachable, high-energy, confident but warm (not aggressive/clickbait-angry by default).

## Visual signature
- **Colors:** ORANGE is the signature/brand color — orange t-shirt and orange hoodie
  recur. Warm orange-to-red gradient backdrops with a glowing halo/rim light behind the
  head. Lean into orange + warm reds; high warmth, rich saturation.
- **Lighting:** dramatic warm rim/halo light behind the subject; moody dark edges with a
  bright warm center. Cinematic, premium feel.
- **Fonts / text style:** bold heavy sans, ≤4 words, one idea. Tech mode → lowercase /
  slash-command style ("/free", "i almost didn't switch :)"). Money mode → heavy ALL-CAPS
  ("FULL BREAKDOWN") + a concrete number. White or high-contrast text; orange for the
  accent/arrow.
- **Composition habits:** strong centered or right-third face, lots of presence, eye
  contact with camera.
- **Backgrounds:** either a clean warm gradient studio glow, or textured exposed brick
  with warm practical lighting (creator/vlog look).

## Design system (applied principles — see references/thumbnail-principles.md)
- **Palette (locked):** main = orange `#FF7300`; plus black, white, mid-grey; **highlight =
  varies per video** to stay fresh while recognisable. For this channel the green/yellow/red
  status colours also recur (agency client-health theme).
- **Font:** keep ONE bold, distinctive, non-generic font across all thumbnails. _TODO: Leo to
  pick/confirm a signature font (the framework's example used "Eastman")._
- **Composition:** rule of thirds — face on one third, hook text on another, hero element on a line.
- **Combine 2–3 thumbnail types per concept** (e.g. creator + big-number + comparison) and
  name them. Hold a style for ~5 videos for recognition, then evolve.
- **Inspiration:** borrow from OTHER niches, not agency-niche clichés — stand out on the feed.
- **Word choice:** big number / contrarian statement / plain title / or blank. ≤4 words.
- **Always gut-check legibility at small size** (imagine it on the crowded homepage).

## Thumbnail modes (decided with creator)
**DEFAULT to Mode C (cinematic).** The graphic modes A/B are secondary now. See full recipe in
`memory/winning-style.md`.
- **Mode C — Cinematic-quality DESIGNED thumbnail (PREFERRED / default):** cinematic *subject
  quality* (crisp, dramatic rim/side lighting, 4K, slight vignette, punchy highlights) composited
  into an actual **designed thumbnail** — clean black/charcoal or bold orange-gradient background,
  bold graphic text (one word orange/red), and ONE simple clean graphic element (traffic light,
  arrow, client card, big number). Reference: **Hormozi / Jeremy Haines / Bing** agency thumbnails.
  - **NOT full cinematic scenes/environments** (no empty boardrooms, streets, windows — that
    leans too "movie still"). Studio-light the person + designed background.
  - Keep big genuine emotion + expressive face. Avoid cluttered icon grids / gibberish dashboards.
- **Mode A — Clean Tech:** light/clean bg, product UI screenshot as hero, lowercase /
  slash hooks, glowing logos, benchmark bars + big %s. Use for AI / Claude / tooling /
  "X just dropped" videos.
- **Mode B — Bold Money:** dark high-contrast bg, concrete $ figure, heavy ALL-CAPS,
  "→ ??? → PROFIT" flow framing, red/green charts, pointing/thinking poses. Use for
  agency / sales / money-model / "I fixed a $Xm business" videos.
- **Orange = decide per concept.** Don't default to the orange-gradient studio backdrop
  from the headshots. Show variants — orange-as-accent (inspo backgrounds) AND
  orange-background — and let the creator choose which reads better for that video.
- Constant brand thread in both modes: one idea, ≤4 words, strong expression + eye
  contact, and a single orange curved arrow pointing at the key thing.

## Do's
- Keep the orange wardrobe + orange accent/arrow — it's the brand thread.
- Use a clear, readable facial expression with strong eye contact.
- Premium, warm, cinematic lighting (the halo/rim glow looks great).
- One concrete number or a curiosity gap ("???", "almost didn't", "too good").

## Don'ts
- No clutter: more than ~4 words of text, or competing focal points.
- No weak/neutral expression — the face must sell an emotion (shock, intensity, concern, pride).
- Don't auto-use the orange-gradient studio background for every thumbnail.
- **No minimal/faceless/empty-object thumbnails.** Leo's face must be PROMINENT and expressive —
  never a tiny corner inset, never big dead negative space, never an object-only hero. The
  "minimal" type suits horror/story channels, not this agency/authority channel. (See
  dislikes/09-minimal-faceless-traffic-light.jpg.)
- Faces should be reasonably **large and dramatically lit** (warm rim light), per the top
  agency references — not small, flat, or stock-calm.
