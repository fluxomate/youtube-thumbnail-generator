# Thumbnail design principles (field guide)

General craft rules distilled from a "how to actually make a thumbnail that gets clicks"
breakdown. Apply these when drafting concepts and image prompts. Pair with `prompting.md`
(prompt mechanics), `memory/example-concepts.md` (the creator's style), and `inspiration/`.

## 1. Composition — rule of thirds (the #1 fix)
Divide the frame with 2 vertical + 2 horizontal lines (a 3×3 grid) and put the key objects —
**face, hero element, and text — ON those lines / at their intersections.** Nearly every top
thumbnail follows this; it turns a messy layout into a clean one. Our default: **face on one
third, hook text on another**, hero element on a line.

## 2. Color & branding — a simple, consistent palette
Skip color-wheel agonising. Build ONE reusable palette and use it on every thumbnail so the
channel is instantly recognisable:
- 1 **main brand colour** (Leo = orange `#FF7300`)
- **black** + **white** + one **mid-tone grey**
- 1 **highlight/accent** colour to make key things pop
To avoid sameness across videos, **change only the highlight colour** per video — fresh but
still on-brand.

## 3. Typography — one consistent, non-generic font
Pick a bold, distinctive (not overused) font and keep it across all thumbnails. Consistency
beats novelty. (Leo: lock a signature font — see style-profile.)

## 4. Thumbnail TYPES — the ~10 that work 99% of the time
- **Shock / emotion:** the most shocking moment + big reaction. Stop the scroll (MrBeast, Airrack).
- **Big things / big numbers:** $50M, 4X, 300+ — scale grabs attention.
- **Simple / minimal:** one screenshot + a curiosity question; stands out by being calm amid
  loud thumbnails (Dr Insanity, Zack).
- **Social hacking:** a famous face from your niche — ONLY if the video is genuinely about
  them, else it's clickbait.
- **Creator + something bigger:** you beside a larger object/element + a context line of text
  at the top. (Leo's main style.)
- **Comparison:** before/after, cheap vs expensive, X vs Y, human vs AI.
- **Blur / hidden:** blur the key person or the final result so people must click to find out.
- **Branded / weird object:** a recognisable logo, or an odd object that triggers "what's that?".

## 5. COMBINE 2–3 formulas (the real secret)
Viral thumbnails stack formulas — e.g. MrWhoseTheBoss = big-number + comparison + social-
hacking at once. When drafting concepts, **deliberately combine 2–3 types** and name which.
Pick 2–3 styles, hold them for ~5 videos so the audience learns your look, then evolve (don't
run the identical formula 100× — it goes stale, except reaction channels).

## 6. Inspiration — steal from OTHER niches, not your own
Copying thumbnails *inside* your niche makes you a copycat: same audience, same look, no
reason to click your new video over an established one. Instead pull layout/effect ideas from
channels in **different niches** and adapt — your thumbnail then stands out in your niche.
(This is why the `inspiration/` library is kept broad.) Keep core brand elements consistent.

## 7. Word choice (the text)
Pick what fits, don't force it: a **big number**; a **bold/contrarian statement** (opposite of
a common belief); a plain **title that represents the video**; or **leave it blank** if nothing
adds — the image can carry it. Keep to **≤4 words** regardless.

## 8. Visual effects / finishing (after the AI base, in Photoshop/Canva)
The AI gives a base; finishing makes it pop. Note these in concepts so the creator knows the
post-steps:
- **Sketch the concept first**, then build.
- **Fancy text:** inner/outer glow, hard outline, drop shadow; save as a reusable style.
- **Warp/bend** elements (e.g. a chart) for energy.
- **Darken a busy background behind text:** new layer, big soft brush, ~5% flow, paint dark
  behind text/character so the text stays readable. Readability beats everything.
- **Recolour** background via hue/saturation to match the palette.
- **Light reflections** on text (overlay blending) so it sits naturally in the scene.
- **Blur** (gaussian) or **mosaic/pixelate** a background that competes with the subject.
- Final **colour grade** (e.g. Lightroom) for mood.

## 9. TEST before publishing
Drop the title + thumbnail into a YouTube-homepage mockup (e.g. thumbsup.tv) and check it
*surrounded by other thumbnails at small size*. If it pops and reads clearly → ship; if not →
back to the drawing board. Always sanity-check legibility at phone size.

## Apply this when generating concepts
- Compose on the rule of thirds (face one third, hook another, hero on a line).
- Make each concept **combine 2–3 named types**.
- Respect the brand palette; vary only the highlight colour.
- Prefer **cross-niche** inspiration over niche clichés.
- Text ≤4 words; remember "blank is fine."
- Note the finishing (blur/darken/warp/composite) to do after generation.
- Picture it small among other thumbnails — is it still readable and distinct?
